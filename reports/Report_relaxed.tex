{\small\noindent\textbf{Abstract.~}Gribben et al.\ report that during human chronic liver disease (MASLD) hepatocytes progressively lose their spatial zonation and acquire cholangiocyte-like (biliary) features. We re-examined the single-nucleus RNA-seq evidence for the \emph{progressive de-zonation} part of that claim. The complication we found is structural: \textbf{disease stage is collinear with how the tissue was obtained.} Healthy and end-stage samples are deceased-donor organ tissue (a $\sim$1\,cm$^3$ cube / explant); the F0--F4 disease spectrum is 16-gauge needle biopsy. So ``healthy $\to$ MASLD $\to$ end-stage'' is also ``organ cube $\to$ needle $\to$ explant,'' and a single pooled correlation cannot tell disease from acquisition. After setting aside the source-confounded endpoints and re-testing on the matched needle-biopsy axis --- using raw UMI counts, depth-normalized per nucleus, with the \textbf{donor} ($\sim$47) as the unit of inference --- we find \textbf{no transcriptional de-zonation signal across fibrosis F1$\to$cirrhotic F4}: cells keep their pericentral/periportal identity, and a large coordinated shift is affirmatively excluded. A per-gene pseudobulk differential-expression scan finds no zonation gene moving (GLUL F4-vs-F1 FDR 0.80); its one per-gene signal is a small \textbf{biliary/ductular-marker rise} at F4, more consistent with cholangiocyte ambient RNA plus rare mixed/doublet nuclei (the ductular reaction) than with broad hepatocyte transdifferentiation. Pre-specified gene-set tests (CAMERA/ROAST/GSEA) plus a per-cell measure then reveal the one real biopsy-internal change: the \textbf{pericentral detox program dims} --- detox transcripts within pericentral nuclei decline across fibrosis (donor-level Spearman $\rho=-0.48$, $p=0.003$), a \emph{relative} decline in level while the cells keep their identity, i.e.\ functional dimming, not de-zonation. We do not claim to overturn the paper; biliary source attribution is \textbf{a lead, not closed.}}\par\vspace{0.5em}

\section{Question and scope}

The published claim has two parts: hepatocytes progressively \emph{lose zonation}, and they acquire \emph{cholangiocyte-like plasticity}. The paper tests de-zonation with a marker--marker correlation (Welch's t-test) pooled across all 47 donors, and confirms plasticity with tissue imaging.

Our question is narrow: \textbf{does the single-nucleus transcriptional evidence for progressive de-zonation survive a matched-source analysis?} Scope: raw snRNA counts only. We do not touch the paper's imaging, immunostaining, protein, organoid, or spatial-architecture evidence --- and we agree with the paper that its strong signal is an end-stage phenomenon. We are not claiming to overturn the full paper.

Zonation means hepatocytes run different programs by lobule position: pericentral (PC, near the central vein) cells favor \emph{GLUL, CYP3A4} and drug-metabolizing enzymes; periportal (PP) cells favor \emph{ASS1, PCK1, HAL, ALDOB} (urea cycle, gluconeogenesis). De-zonation could break this in several distinct ways --- PC cells deplete, zonal genes turn off, cells co-express both programs, the gradient compresses, or the PP:PC balance shifts. Because each failure mode has a different count signature, we tested them separately rather than leaning on one global score.

Both \emph{ends} of the apparent disease axis are organ tissue; only the F0--F4 middle is needle biopsy (grounded directly in Paper 1's Methods, \texttt{papers/s41586-024-07465-2.pdf}; see \texttt{reports/SYNTHESIS.md} \S1a). The trajectory ``healthy $\to$ MASLD $\to$ end-stage'' is therefore also an \textbf{acquisition trajectory} --- ischemia, procurement delay, dissociation, organ-cube-vs-needle, and sequencing batch all move together with stage (run almost perfectly predicts tissue source: Cram\'er's V $\approx 0.84$). This is the load-bearing issue: it motivates excluding the ends and re-testing biopsy-only.

\section{Methods that matter}

Only the choices that explain why our result differs.

\textbf{3.1 Raw counts, not the shipped matrix.} The distributed matrix is an SCT model output (depth squeezed); we extracted real RNA-assay UMI counts. All molecule inference is on raw counts.

\textbf{3.2 Depth-normalized anchor classification.} Each hepatocyte nucleus is down-thinned to a common 1{,}500-UMI budget (binomial thinning, averaged over draws) so detection reflects biology not depth. A marker is ``on'' at $\ge 2$ UMI (ambient-robust). Using strict mutually-exclusive anchors --- PC = \emph{GLUL/CYP3A4}, PP = \emph{ASS1/PCK1/HAL/ALDOB} --- each nucleus is labeled PC, PP, dual, or null. The unit of inference is the \textbf{donor}, never the cell. (This is differential-abundance / composition analysis; we call it ``anchor classification.'')

\textbf{3.3 Donor-level pseudobulk DGE.} Sum each donor's hepatocyte raw UMIs into one count profile; run edgeR (TMM normalization, negative-binomial quasi-likelihood), primary contrast F4-vs-F1 across 38 biopsy donors. Purpose: a genome-wide check for \textbf{non-zonation} programs given that zonation structure is treated as settled --- not a second de-zonation test.

\section{Results}

\subsection{R1 --- The endpoints carry acquisition stress, so we exclude them}
A curated stress module (immediate-early + heat-shock genes), depth-normalized, runs at \textbf{0.074} mean UMIs/nucleus in biopsies, \textbf{0.282} in healthy organ cubes, and \textbf{1.675} in end-stage explants ($\sim$22$\times$ biopsy). The decisive control is cross-lineage: end-stage-vs-biopsy stress is $\sim$18.5$\times$ in hepatocytes and $\sim$18.2$\times$ in \textbf{endothelial cells}, which carry no zonation program. A signal identical in a non-zonated lineage is whole-organ procurement handling, not hepatocyte biology --- and it perturbs exactly the oxygen/Wnt-gradient axis the de-zonation claim rests on. This justifies excluding the source-confounded endpoints from the disease axis.

\subsection{R2 --- On the matched biopsy axis, there is no de-zonation signal}
Across F0--F4 needle biopsies every cell-redistribution failure mode comes up flat:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
\item \textbf{Depletion:} PC-anchor fraction 36/19/23/22/21\% (F0--F4) --- flat/non-monotone.
\item \textbf{Co-expression:} the dual fraction looks inflated ($\sim$7--10\%) only at a 1-UMI threshold; at the ambient-robust $\ge 2$-UMI cut it collapses to \textbf{$\sim$0.2--0.6\%} with no trend --- the 1-UMI signal was ambient soup.
\item \textbf{Turn-off:} null fraction flat.
\item \textbf{Composition:} PP:PC ratio 0.62/1.16/1.01/1.10/1.18 --- flat/non-monotone.
\end{itemize}

The poles stay dominant; at most a mild non-monotone middle-ward drift. This is no de-zonation \emph{signal} --- not a positive proof that zonation is ``preserved,'' only that no de-zonation is detectable above the donor-level scatter. The null is robust across the full sensitivity grid and a marker-set ladder from 2 to 1{,}637 genes (including Paper 2's own 40 landmark genes). A per-cell view of the same data (the pericentral$\leftrightarrow$periportal balance of each nucleus) tells the same story: across biopsy F1$\to$F4 the cells stay spread along the gradient, with only the confounded explant collapsing to a single pole.

\subsection{R3 --- Robustness and an honest bound}
The result is stable across the sensitivity grid (depth budgets 1{,}000/1{,}500/3{,}000; GLUL-only/CYP3A4-only/both; Monte-Carlo SD on donor medians 0.006--0.010). An affirmative equivalence test (TOST, 90\% CI, donor-level anchor fractions) puts the F4-vs-F1 PC-anchor change at \textbf{+0.024 (90\% CI $-0.147$ to $+0.194$)}. So we \textbf{exclude a large shift on the order of 20 percentage points} (the well-powered F1$\to$F3 interior tightens to $\sim\pm0.12$); we do \textbf{not} exclude a subtle drift of $\le 10$ points (donor SD is large at F4 n=4). Large re-zonation is ruled out; subtle drift is not.

\subsection{R4 --- The only per-gene DGE signal is a biliary rise, likely compositional}
Genome-wide, of $\sim$21{,}000 genes only \textbf{64} reach FDR $< 0.05$ at F4-vs-F1, and they are a biliary/ductular set --- \emph{EPCAM, GRHL2, SPINT2, SOX4, SOX9, B3GNT3} --- plus \emph{CXCL10}. Zonation genes are flat at the expression level (\textbf{GLUL F4-vs-F1 FDR 0.80 --- well above the 0.05 cutoff, so not significant}; all detox genes FDR $> 0.79$), an independent count-based check agreeing with the anchor classification. So the per-gene discovery scan finds \textbf{a biliary-marker rise inside hepatocyte-labeled pseudobulk, not zonation loss.}

Source attribution (a side lead): the headline genes are 5--78$\times$ more abundant in cholangiocytes than hepatocytes; the cholangiocyte fraction rises to $\sim$8\% at F4 (the ductular reaction); only $\sim$0.4\% of hepatocyte nuclei robustly co-express $\ge 2$ biliary markers (a prevalence over \emph{all} hepatocytes --- it argues against \emph{mass} transdifferentiation but does not exclude a rare sub-population); ambient correction (decontX) halves the hits 64$\to$34 and drops \emph{SOX4/SOX9} below significance. But \emph{EPCAM/B3GNT3/SPINT2} survive (decontX is a consistency check, not a verdict, so a rare intrinsic biliary state is not excluded), and \emph{CXCL10} is separate --- its hepatocyte expression does not track the cholangiocyte fraction (corr $-0.09$), so it is a candidate real inflammatory signal, not ambient. Careful conclusion: the biliary signal is \textbf{real in hepatocyte-labeled pseudobulk, source not resolved}, and the weight of evidence favors \textbf{ambient RNA + rare mixed/doublet nuclei} over broad transdifferentiation --- a lead, not a closed verdict.

\subsection{R5 --- The one real change: the pericentral detox program dims}
A per-gene scan is blind to a weak \emph{coordinated} program --- many genes each drifting a little in the same direction, none crossing FDR alone. Pre-specified gene-set tests are built for exactly that. Over ten sets fixed in advance (pericentral identity and detox, periportal identity and urea cycle, bile-acid/lipid, cholangiocyte, plus interferon / fibrogenesis / ER-stress \emph{controls} whose answers biology already dictates), three independent methods agree: \texttt{camera} (competitive), \texttt{mroast} (self-contained), and GSEA. The \textbf{pericentral detox programs go down} with fibrosis --- phase-I CYP detox (camera FDR $2\times10^{-6}$), the pericentral landmark set (Paper 2 landmarks, FDR $1.5\times10^{-4}$), phase-II conjugation (FDR $7\times10^{-3}$) --- while the controls behave exactly as they must (fibrogenesis and inflammation up, ER-stress flat), which is what tells us the test is reading real biology, not noise.

Because a competitive test on relative data could be fooled by other genes inflating (a ``pie-slice'' artifact), we checked adversarially: the result survives composition-robust TMM normalization, survives the self-contained \texttt{mroast} test (the CYP detox core clears alone), survives dropping the up-going genes --- and, decisively, a \textbf{per-cell measure} that composition cannot touch confirms it. Counting detox transcripts per pericentral nucleus after equalizing read depth (down-thinning every nucleus to 1{,}500 UMIs), the level falls steadily across fibrosis: $\sim$11.9 (F1) to $\sim$8.8 (F4) transcripts per nucleus, a donor-level Spearman $\rho=-0.48$ ($p=0.003$).

This is \textbf{dimming}, not de-zonation. Like a radio: the station did not change genre (the cells keep their pericentral \emph{class} --- the anchor fractions in R2 stay flat), but the volume dropped on the detox channel. The robust decline is the cytochrome-P450 core --- the only set that also moves on its own in the self-contained test; the broader landmark and phase-II sets lean down only faintly (competitive-only, no single gene significant), too weak to call a turn-off. What holds the class is the two \emph{gating} anchors GLUL and CYP3A4 (a yes/no detection call), individually flat (per-gene FDR 0.80, 0.85), so the anchor fraction stays put. Three honest caveats: this is a \textbf{relative} decline (detox transcripts within a fixed sequencing budget, not proven absolute molecule loss); it is a donor-level trend; and it could partly reflect a shift among pericentral subzones rather than pure within-cell downregulation. It does \emph{not} rescue the paper's identity-loss claim.

\section{Interpretation}

\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
\item \textbf{The full trajectory is source-confounded} --- do not read healthy $\to$ end-stage as a clean continuation of the disease axis. The endpoints are organ tissue carrying organ-wide procurement stress.
\item \textbf{In matched biopsies, there is no transcriptional de-zonation signal} --- every cell-redistribution failure mode (depletion, co-expression, turn-off, composition shift) is flat across F1$\to$cirrhotic F4, and a large coordinated shift is affirmatively excluded. The cells keep their zonal identity.
\item \textbf{The one real biopsy-internal change is dimming, not de-zonation} --- the pericentral detox program declines in \emph{level} (gene-set down-regulation plus a per-cell depth-matched trend, $\rho=-0.48$, $p=0.003$) while the cells retain their pericentral identity. This is a relative decline in a zonated program's output, not a loss of identity or a transdifferentiation.
\item \textbf{The F4 biliary/ductular signal is real but source-uncertain} --- most consistent with cholangiocyte ambient RNA plus rare mixed/doublet nuclei, with \emph{CXCL10} a possible real hepatocyte inflammatory exception. This source-attribution leg is a promising lead, not a closed finding.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Limitations and next steps}

Stated plainly, not apologetically. This is a hackathon-scale re-analysis: no wet-lab, no imaging or spatial re-analysis, single cohort. The F4 biopsy stratum is thin (n=4), so subtle effects there are underpowered --- which is why we lead the dimming result with the fibrosis-wide trend, not the F1-vs-F4 endpoint. The detox-dimming finding itself is a \emph{relative} decline within a fixed sequencing budget (not proven absolute molecule loss), a donor-level trend, and could partly reflect a shift among pericentral subzones; in the strictly self-contained gene-set test only the CYP detox core clears alone. decontX is a sensitivity check using the paper's own labels (mild circularity), not proof of ambient origin. And anchor markers simplify what is really a continuous gradient; snRNA measures per-cell transcriptional balance, not lobule geometry --- we report the absence of a transcriptional de-zonation \emph{signal}, not spatial/architectural preservation.

Next steps: validate the detox-dimming gradient in spatial / independent-biopsy tissue and with a functional readout of pericentral detox capacity (and settle within-cell downregulation vs pericentral-subzone shift); leave-one-F4-donor-out DGE on the biliary hits; a quantitative contamination model asking whether the ambient/doublet load numerically explains the counts; and an independent MASLD biopsy cohort. (The gene-set/pathway tests --- CAMERA, ROAST, GSEA --- that close the weak-coordinated-program gap are reported in R5.)

\item \textbf{Why we abandoned the relative ruler.} An early z-scored ``zonation coordinate'' produced an apparent collapse. It was abandoned for two reasons: (a) it pools across the source confound, so a between-group acquisition discontinuity reads as within-group de-zonation (a Simpson's-paradox reversal --- each healthy donor is individually zonated, anti-corr $\approx -0.21$, yet pooling flips the sign to $\approx +0.01$); and (b) it is intrinsically depth-sensitive and mechanism-blind --- turn-off, co-expression, and noise all shrink its spread alike.
