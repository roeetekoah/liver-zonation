# F10 — What Paper 1 actually claims, and exactly what we can/cannot say about it

**Status: LIVE — verified directly from the PDF (`papers/s41586-024-07465-2.pdf`), not from any prose doc.**
This bounds our contribution so we neither under- nor over-claim.

## Verified quotes (Paper 1 = Gribben et al., Nature 2024)
- Headline framing: *"Hepatocytes lose their zonation and considerable reorganization of the biliary tree
  takes place. **More importantly**, our study uncovers transdifferentiation…"* → **de-zonation is a
  SUPPORTING observation; hepatocyte↔cholangiocyte plasticity is the headline.**
- Claim scope: *"…indicating a loss of zonation at the **transcriptional and protein level** in hepatocytes."*
  → they claim it at BOTH levels.
- Their snRNA method: *"Loss of zonation through disease was assessed by **comparing the correlation between
  pairs of periportal and pericentral markers**, which were then contrasted using **Welch's t-test**."*
  → the relative marker–marker **correlation statistic** — exactly the fragile approach we abandoned.
- Their test pools the confounded axis: the Welch's t-test is over *"n = 47 biologically independent donors:
  healthy, 4; NAFLD, 7; NASH, 27; cirrhosis, 4; end stage, 5"* → **mixes biopsy + healthy + explant** (our
  tissue-source confound runs straight through their significance test).

## Paper 1's three evidence legs for de-zonation (all centered on end-stage explant tissue)
1. **snRNA marker-correlation breakdown** (Fig 2b) — the transcriptional leg.
2. **Immunofluorescence** — GLUL (PC) + ASS1 (PP) co-staining in single cells (Fig 2c) — protein leg.
3. **FLASH 3D imaging** of cleared whole organs (Fig 2d,e) — architectural leg.

## What WE can and cannot say (the honest bound)
- **We attack leg #1 only:** the snRNA/transcriptional evidence is a relative-correlation statistic computed
  on tissue-source-confounded data (biopsy vs deceased-donor explant, with uncontrolled organ-wide
  procurement stress — F2/F5). Across the **acquisition-matched biopsy disease axis (F1→cirrhotic F4)** there
  is **no transcriptional de-zonation** (F1, F8, F9). So: *"the snRNA/transcriptional evidence for MASLD
  de-zonation is a confounded correlation artifact, and there is no gene-expression de-zonation across the
  acquisition-matched biopsy disease axis"* (D3).
- **We CANNOT refute legs #2–3** (protein co-staining, 3D architecture). End-stage cirrhosis genuinely
  destroys lobular architecture; snRNA measures per-cell transcriptional balance, not geometry (the F9
  architecture caveat).
- **We do NOT overturn the paper** — its headline is plasticity, and de-zonation is supporting. Our result is
  a **valid correction of one evidence leg + the progressive-disease framing**, real and publishable, not a
  debunking of the whole claim or the imaging.
